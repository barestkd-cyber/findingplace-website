A Day at The Finding Place

Calm rhythms, generous outdoor time, and real work for little hands.

Who We Serve

We keep our group small and mixed in age on purpose. When a three-year-old and a seven-year-old share the same days, the younger ones learn by watching, the older ones grow by helping, and every child is met where they actually are instead of where a grade level says they should be. That kind of room only works when it stays small, so we hold just sixteen spaces, ages three through third grade. If that sounds like the childhood you want for your family, we'd love for you to reach out.

Our Daily Rhythm

Young children thrive on rhythm more than rigid schedules. Our days follow a predictable flow that still leaves room to chase the morning's wonder, whatever it turns out to be.

Learning at Their Own Pace

There is no bell here that says everyone must be on the same page at the same time. In a mixed-age room, a child can be reading well beyond their years and still take their time with numbers, or the other way around, and neither one is a problem to fix. It is simply where they are that week.

That freedom takes the pressure off. Children aren't labeled ahead or behind. They aren't hurried through something they haven't finished or held back from something they're ready for. They get to move at the pace their own mind is setting.

It is the same idea that runs through everything we do: we follow the child.

What Your Child Will Gain

  • Independence: dressing themselves, managing belongings, completing real tasks start to finish.
  • Kindergarten readiness: letters, numbers, fine motor strength, and listening skills — grown naturally through play and hands-on work.
  • Social confidence: sharing, turn-taking, resolving conflicts with words, and caring for younger friends.
  • A relationship with nature: comfort, curiosity, and confidence outdoors in every season.
  • Love of learning: the one that matters most. Children who leave us still asking "why?" with sparkling eyes.
