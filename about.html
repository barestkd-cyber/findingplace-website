Our Story

The Finding Place started with a simple belief: childhood deserves room to breathe.

Why We Exist

[Founder's name] founded The Finding Place because she kept seeing the same thing: bright, curious little children being rushed — rushed through their days, rushed toward academics, rushed past the very experiences that build strong, kind, capable people.

She wanted to create something different for Tyler families. Not a bigger program with more enrollment and more noise, but a smaller one. A place where a child can spend twenty uninterrupted minutes watching a ladybug. A place where teachers know not just every child's name, but their stories, their fears, their jokes.

What We Believe

  • Children are capable. Given real responsibility and real respect, young children rise to it — beautifully.
  • Nature is a teacher. The East Texas outdoors offers daily lessons no curriculum can replicate.
  • Small is a feature, not a limitation. Low numbers mean deep relationships, calm days, and genuine attention.
  • Families are partners. You will always know how your child is doing, what they're working on, and what they loved that day.
  • Play is serious learning. Counting, language, science, empathy — it's all happening inside play, every day.
"It is not that the child should be taught less — it is that the child should be trusted more."

Inspired By the Best Ideas in Early Childhood

We aren't loyal to a single brand of education. We draw from the Montessori tradition — independence, hands-on materials, the prepared environment — and from nature-based education, where the outdoors is a daily classroom. We're also influenced by the Reggio Emilia approach and its conviction that children express their understanding in a hundred different languages: building, painting, storytelling, pretending.

What ties it together is simple: we follow the child.
