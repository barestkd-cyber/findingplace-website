Our Story

The Finding Place started with a simple belief: childhood deserves room to breathe.

Why We Exist

Michele Bares started The Finding Place with her husband, Matt Bares, because she wanted her own grandchildren to have the kind of childhood her children had: fun, peaceful, child-led, and genuinely magical. She homeschooled all of her own kids, and she wanted to open that same magic up to other families, too.

Not a bigger program with more enrollment and more noise, but a smaller one. A place where a child can spend twenty uninterrupted minutes watching a ladybug. A place where a guide knows not just every child's name, but their stories, their ideas, and their questions.

What We Believe

  • Children are capable. Given real responsibility and real respect, young children rise to it — beautifully.
  • Nature is a teacher. The East Texas outdoors offers daily lessons no curriculum can replicate.
  • Small is a feature, not a limitation. Low numbers mean deep relationships, calm days, and genuine attention.
  • Families are partners. You will always know how your child is doing, what they're working on, and what they loved that day.
  • Play is serious learning. Counting, language, science, empathy — it's all happening inside play, every day.
"I'm not a teacher. I'm a guide. The children lead, and I follow their cues." — Mimzy

Inspired By the Best Ideas in Early Childhood

We draw deeply from the Reggio Emilia approach, the belief that every child is capable and curious, that learning should follow the child's own questions, and that children express their understanding in a hundred different languages: building, painting, storytelling, pretending. We pair it with nature-based education, where the East Texas outdoors is a daily classroom.

What ties it together is simple: we follow the child.

Faith at The Finding Place

We are a Christian school, and our faith is woven gently into our days. We pray together, we begin our mornings with worship, and we share the love of Jesus in ways that are warm and age-appropriate. Families of all backgrounds are welcome here.

Michele "Mimzy" Bares

Michele, known as Mimzy to the children, has led The Finding Place for eight years. She holds a degree from Lamar University and has spent thirty-five years homeschooling her own children and other families' children. She is also a third-degree black belt in Taekwondo and has taught children martial arts for more than twenty years.

She sees her role a little differently than most. She is not there to run the children through a plan. She is there to watch, to listen, and to add what each child needs, when they need it.
